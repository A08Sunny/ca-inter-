## 1. Exam Structure & Syllabus Overview

### New Scheme: 6 Papers, 2 Groups (100 marks each)

| Group | Paper | Subject | Exam Pattern |
|-------|-------|---------|-------------|
| **I** | 1 | Advanced Accounting | 70% Descriptive + 30% MCQ |
| **I** | 2 | Corporate and Other Laws | 70% Descriptive + 30% MCQ |
| **I** | 3 | Taxation (IT 50 + GST 50) | 70% Descriptive + 30% MCQ |
| **II** | 4 | Cost & Management Accounting | 70% Descriptive + 30% MCQ |
| **II** | 5 | Auditing & Ethics | 70% Descriptive + 30% MCQ |
| **II** | 6 | FM (50) + SM (50) | 70% Descriptive + 30% MCQ |

### Passing Criteria
- **Minimum 40%** in each individual paper
- **Minimum 50% aggregate** in each group
- **For AIR:** Both groups in one attempt, generally 75%+ total

## 2. Which Subject to Start First?

### ✅ Recommended Starting Order

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The golden rule: **Start with practical subjects first, add theory subjects later.** Practical subjects need consistent practice; theory subjects need frequent revision closer to exams.

#### If preparing for **Both Groups together** (recommended for AIR):

| Phase | Subjects to Start | Why |
|-------|------------------|-----|
| **Month 1-2** | Advanced Accounting + Costing | Both are practical, need strong foundation & daily practice |
| **Month 2-3** | Add Taxation (IT first, then GST) | Practical + amendments come later; start early |
| **Month 3-4** | Add FM (Financial Management) | Practical, formula-heavy, needs time |
| **Month 4-5** | Add Corporate Law + Auditing | Theory-heavy, retain better when closer to exam |
| **Month 5-6** | Add SM (Strategic Management) | Conceptual theory, can be done in 3-4 weeks |
| **Month 6-8** | Full Revision + Mocks | All subjects, rotating revision |

#### If preparing for **One Group at a time**:

- **Group 1 first:** Start with Advanced Accounting → Taxation → Law
- **Group 2 first:** Start with Costing → FM → Auditing → SM

### 📘 Paper 1: Advanced Accounting (100 Marks)

**Nature:** Practical (85%) + Theory (15%) | **Difficulty:** High | **Scoring:** High if practiced well

#### Chapter Breakdown & Weightage:

| # | Chapter | Weightage | Priority | Strategy |
|---|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Accounting Standards & Framework | **55-65%** | 🔴 Critical | Master AS 1-29 conceptually; practice 5+ sums daily from each AS |
| 2 | Company Accounts (Schedule III) | **30-35%** | 🔴 Critical | Learn Schedule III format by heart; practice financial statement preparation |
| 3 | Amalgamation, Absorption & Reconstruction | Part of above | 🔴 Critical | Focus on purchase consideration methods; practice complex sums |
| 4 | Consolidation of Financial Statements | Part of above | 🟡 Important | Intercompany eliminations, minority interest — practice methodically |
| 5 | Banking, Insurance, Electricity Companies | **5-10%** | 🟢 Moderate | Unique formats — learn once, revise thrice |
| 6 | Accounting for Foreign Branches | **5-10%** | 🟢 Moderate | Fixed exchange rate vs. closing rate — practice conversion problems |

**Study Approach:**
1. Read ICAI Study Material chapter → Make formula/format sheet
2. Solve ICAI Practice Manual examples (100%)
3. Solve past 5 years' RTP & MTP questions (chapter-wise)
4. Do one mock test per week (timed)

### 📗 Paper 2: Corporate and Other Laws (100 Marks)

**Nature:** Theory (90%) + Case Study (10%) | **Difficulty:** Medium | **Scoring:** Moderate

#### Chapter Breakdown & Weightage:

| # | Section | Weightage | Priority | Strategy |
|---|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Part I: Company Law + LLP (70 Marks)** |
| 1 | Preliminary (Definitions & Interpretation) | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Understand key definitions, memorize sections |
| 2 | Incorporation & Matters Incidental | 8-10% | 🔴 Critical | MOA, AOA, One Person Company |
| 3 | Prospectus & Allotment of Securities | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | SEBI guidelines, private placement, types of prospectus |
| 4 | Share Capital & Debentures | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | Issue, transfer, buyback — section numbers essential |
| 5 | Management & Administration | 12-15% | 🔴 Critical | Directors, KMP, Board meetings, AGM/EGM |
| 6 | Accounts, Audit & Dividends | 8-10% | 🟡 Important | Sec 128-138, dividend provisions |
| 7 | LLP Act, 2008 | 5-7% | 🟢 Moderate | Basics — formation, agreement, liability |
| **Part II: Other Laws (30 Marks)** |
| 8 | Indian Contract Act, 1872 | 8-10% | 🔴 Critical | Offer, acceptance, consideration, breach |
| 9 | Sale of Goods Act, 1930 | 8-10% | 🟡 Important | Conditions vs warranties, transfer of title |
| 10 | Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 | 5-7% | 🟡 Important | Types, dishonor, holder in due course |
| 11 | Consumer Protection Act, 2019 | 3-5% | 🟢 Moderate | Rights, redressal forums |

**Study Approach:**
1. Read bare act provisions alongside ICAI material
2. Prepare **section-wise short notes** with keywords
3. Practice **case studies** (apply law to facts)
4. Revise section numbers 3 times before exam — this is KEY

### 📕 Paper 3: Taxation (100 Marks)

**Nature:** Practical (70%) + Theory (30%) | **Difficulty:** High | **Scoring:** Very High if practiced

#### Section A — Income Tax (50 Marks)

| # | Chapter | Weightage | Priority | Strategy |
|---|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Basic Concepts (Person, AY, PY, Income) | 3-5% | 🟡 Foundation | Must be crystal clear before moving ahead |
| 2 | Residential Status & Scope | 5-7% | 🔴 Critical | ROR, RNOR, NR — practice classification problems |
| 3 | Income from Salary | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | HRA, gratuity, leave encashment sums — daily practice |
| 4 | Income from House Property | 8-10% | 🔴 Critical | Deemed let-out, unrealised rent — formula sheet |
| 5 | Profits & Gains from Business/Profession | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | Sec 28-44, depreciation, deemed profits |
| 6 | Capital Gains | 8-10% | 🔴 Critical | Short-term vs long-term, indexation, exemptions (54/54EC/54F) |
| 7 | Income from Other Sources | 5-7% | 🟡 Important | Sec 56, deemed dividend |
| 8 | Clubbing, Set-off, Carry Forward | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Inter-source & inter-head set-off rules |
| 9 | Deductions (Chapter VI-A) | 5-7% | 🟡 Important | 80C, 80D, 80E — limits & conditions |
| 10 | Computation of Total Income & Tax | 3-5% | 🔴 Critical | Full computation problems — integrate all heads |

#### Section B — GST (50 Marks)

| # | Chapter | Weightage | Priority | Strategy |
|---|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Introduction to GST | 3-5% | 🟢 Moderate | Overview, structure, CGST/SGST/IGST |
| 2 | Supply, Levy & Collection | 15-20% | 🔴 Critical | What constitutes supply, composite vs mixed |
| 3 | Exemptions from GST | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Key exempted services & goods |
| 4 | Time, Place & Value of Supply | 15-20% | 🔴 Critical | Practice determination problems |
| 5 | Input Tax Credit | 10-15% | 🔴 Critical | Eligible/ineligible ITC, reversal rules |
| 6 | Registration | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Threshold limits, compulsory registration |
| 7 | Tax Invoice, Payment & Returns | 5-10% | 🟡 Important | E-way bills, return filing process |

**Study Approach:**
1. Study IT and GST **separately** — don't mix them in same day initially
2. **Practice 10-15 sums daily** during preparation phase
3. Keep an **amendments file** — ICAI releases updates; be current
4. For GST, focus on **section numbers + rules** together

### 📙 Paper 4: Cost & Management Accounting (100 Marks)

**Nature:** Practical (90%) + Theory (10%) | **Difficulty:** Medium-High | **Scoring:** Very High

#### Chapter Breakdown & Weightage:

| # | Chapter | Weightage | Priority | Strategy |
|---|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Overview of CMA | 3-5% | 🟢 Moderate | Definitions, difference between cost & management accounting |
| 2 | Material Cost | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | EOQ, ABC analysis, stock levels — daily sums |
| 3 | Employee Cost | 8-10% | 🟡 Important | Incentive schemes (Halsey, Rowan, Taylor) |
| 4 | Overheads (Absorption & Allocation) | 12-15% | 🔴 Critical | Machine hour rate, reciprocal services — practice heavily |
| 5 | Activity Based Costing (ABC) | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Cost drivers, cost pools — conceptual clarity |
| 6 | Cost Accounting Systems (Job, Batch, Process, Contract) | 15-18% | 🔴 Critical | Process costing (equivalent units), contract costing |
| 7 | Budgeting & Budgetary Control | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | Flexible budget, cash budget — practice formats |
| 8 | Standard Costing & Variance Analysis | 12-15% | 🔴 Critical | Material, Labour, Overhead variances — formula mastery |
| 9 | Marginal Costing | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | BEP, CVP analysis, make or buy decisions |
| 10 | Decision-Making Techniques | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Relevant costs, special order pricing |

**Study Approach:**
1. **Make a master formula sheet** — revise it every morning
2. Practice sums from ICAI material first → then RTP/MTP
3. Focus on **process costing and standard costing** — highest scoring
4. Time yourself while solving; aim for 12-15 minutes per question

### 📓 Paper 5: Auditing & Ethics (100 Marks)

**Nature:** Theory (95%) + Case Study (5%) | **Difficulty:** Medium | **Scoring:** Moderate (depends on writing)

#### Chapter Breakdown & Weightage:

| # | Chapter | Weightage | Priority | Strategy |
|---|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Nature, Objective & Scope of Audit | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Foundation concepts |
| 2 | Audit Concepts, Techniques & Standards | 8-10% | 🔴 Critical | SAs (Standards on Auditing) — learn keywords |
| 3 | Audit Planning, Strategy & Execution | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | Audit planning memorandum, materiality |
| 4 | Risk Assessment & Internal Control | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | Control risk, detection risk, test of controls |
| 5 | Audit Documentation & Evidence | 8-10% | 🟡 Important | Sufficient appropriate evidence, working papers |
| 6 | Company Audit | 15-18% | 🔴 Critical | Sec 139-148, CARO 2020, Audit Report |
| 7 | Audit of Different Entities | 8-10% | 🟡 Important | Government, bank, trust, cooperative audit |
| 8 | Professional Ethics | 10-12% | 🔴 Critical | Code of Ethics, independence, conflicts of interest |
| 9 | Audit Sampling & Analytical Procedures | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Statistical vs non-statistical sampling |
| 10 | SA Numbers (200 series to 720 series) | Across all | 🔴 Critical | Learn SA numbers with keywords — exam tested heavily |

**Study Approach:**
1. **Read ICAI material** — this is the Bible for Audit
2. Prepare **SA-wise summary sheet** with key points and SA numbers
3. Practice **answer writing** — use headings, bullets, keywords
4. Link theory with practical scenarios (case studies from RTPs)
5. **Revise 3 times minimum** — retention is the challenge

| 5 | Capital Budgeting | 12-15% | 🔴 Critical | NPV, IRR, Payback — daily practice |
| 6 | Working Capital Management | 8-10% | 🟡 Important | Operating cycle, receivables/inventory management |
| 7 | Dividend Decisions | 5-8% | 🟡 Important | Walter, Gordon, MM model |

#### Section B — Strategic Management (50 Marks)

| # | Chapter | Weightage | Priority | Strategy |
|---|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Introduction to Strategic Management | 10-12% | 🟡 Important | Vision, mission, objectives, strategic intent |
| 2 | External Environment Analysis | 15-20% | 🔴 Critical | PESTLE, Porter's 5 Forces, competitive analysis |
| 3 | Internal Environment Analysis | 15-20% | 🔴 Critical | SWOT, value chain, BCG matrix, core competence |

1. **FM:** Make formula sheet → Practice 10 sums daily → Master NPV & WACC

3. **SM can be done in 3-4 weeks** if you focus; don't underestimate it though
4. For FM: Solve RTPs under timed conditions

## 9. Revision Strategy

### The 3-Layer Revision System

| Layer | When | How |
|-------|------|-----|
| **Daily Revision** | Every evening | Revise what you studied that day (30-45 min) |
| **Weekly Revision** | Every Sunday | Revise entire week's topics using short notes |
| **Monthly Mega Revision** | End of each month | Full revision of all studied chapters using formula sheets + keyword notes |

### Revision Priority (70-20-10 Rule)

- **70%** → Revise high-weightage, frequently tested topics
- **20%** → Revise moderate-weightage topics
- **10%** → Revise low-weightage topics (don't skip — MCQ questions come from here)

### Note-Making Template for Each Chapter

```
Chapter: _______________          Subject: _______________
Key Concepts:    (3-5 bullet points)
Important Sections/SAs:  (list section or SA numbers)
Formulas:        (all formulas on one page)
Frequently Asked Questions:  (from RTPs/MTPs)
My Weak Areas:   (topics to re-revise)
```

## 10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

> [!CAUTION]
> These are the most common reasons students fail to achieve AIR. Read this carefully.

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ What to Do Instead |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Relying only on coaching classes | Use coaching as supplement; ICAI material is primary |
| Skipping ICAI Study Material | Complete it cover-to-cover, even if boring |
| Not practicing enough sums (practical subjects) | Minimum 10-15 problems/day for Accounting, Tax, Costing, FM |
| Starting theory subjects too early, forgetting by exam | Start theory subjects 3-4 months before exam, revise frequently |
| Taking SM/FMSM lightly | Students lose AIR because of SM; give it proper time |
| Not doing mock tests | Do minimum 5 full mock tests per subject |
| Creating backlogs | Zero tolerance for backlogs; daily completion targets |
| Ignoring amendments | Check ICAI website 2 months before exam for latest amendments |
| Not working on answer presentation | Practice writing structured answers with headings & keywords |
| Studying 14+ hours without breaks | 8-10 quality hours > 14 tired hours. Take breaks, sleep well. |
| Comparing with others | Focus on your own journey. Everyone's pace is different. |
| Not solving RTPs and MTPs | These are the CLOSEST to actual exam questions — solve every single one |
